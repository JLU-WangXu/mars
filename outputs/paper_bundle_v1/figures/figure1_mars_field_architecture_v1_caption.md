# Figure 1 | MARS-FIELD architecture

`MARS-FIELD` projects geometric, phylogenetic, ancestral, retrieval-based, and environment-conditioned evidence into a shared residue energy field. The geometric encoder captures backbone-conditioned compatibility, the phylo-sequence encoder captures conservation and family adaptation statistics, the ancestral lineage encoder represents posterior residue preferences and uncertainty from reconstructed lineage states, the retrieval branch queries a motif atlas of structurally similar local patterns, and the environment branch modulates the field according to engineering-relevant stress objectives. These evidence streams parameterize site-wise residue energies `U(i, a)` and pairwise coupling energies `C(i, j, a, b)`, which are decoded into constrained sequence designs and calibrated before final ranking.

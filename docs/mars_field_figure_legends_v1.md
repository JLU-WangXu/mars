# MARS-FIELD Figure Legends v1

## Figure 1 | MARS-FIELD integrates heterogeneous evidence into a shared residue field

`MARS-FIELD` is organized as a unified evidence-to-sequence controller rather than as a collection of independent proposal engines. Structural geometry, phylogenetic profiles, ancestral lineage information, retrieval-based motif memory, and environment-conditioned engineering context are first encoded as evidence streams and then projected into a shared residue field over the design positions. The native objects of this field are site-wise residue energies `U(i, a)` and pairwise coupling energies `C(i, j, a, b)`, which together define the sequence energy landscape used for controller inference and constrained decoding. The right side of the figure shows the two downstream actions performed on this field: candidate-level calibrated selection and decode-time sequence generation. The lower inset illustrates how this abstract field is instantiated on a concrete target, here represented by a compact residue-field example over selected design positions.

## Figure 2 | Benchmark claim figure

This figure summarizes the benchmark-level claim of the current `MARS-FIELD` implementation. Panel a shows paired policy-score differences between the final controller and the incumbent benchmark on the twelve-target panel. Positive values indicate targets on which the final controller improved the policy score; negative values indicate targets with lower final policy scores. Panel b condenses the benchmark into headline metrics, including the number of improved targets, the number of decreased targets, the mean paired policy delta, and the total number of retained neural-decoder-derived candidates. Panel c decomposes neural decoder activity across targets into preview, rejected, and retained novel candidates, showing that decode-time neural generation is active across the panel but only selectively retained. Panel d summarizes family-level behavior, illustrating that the benchmark spans multiple protein families and prior regimes rather than a single narrow design setting.

## Figure 3 | Mechanism and limitation figure

This figure explains why the current controller behaves as it does and where its limits remain. Panel a summarizes component ablations and shows that oxidation-aware and evolutionary information remain dominant constraints in the present benchmark. Panel b displays target-specific neural gate profiles, showing that different targets draw on distinct mixtures of geometry, phylogeny, ancestry, retrieval, and environment signals. Panel c focuses on the concentrated set of negative paired-delta targets and shows that the current regressions are localized rather than diffuse. Panel d quantifies decoder selectivity by comparing preview counts with retained-novel fractions across targets, illustrating that the neural decoder is not used as a blind novelty generator but as a gated, target-dependent proposal source.

## Figure 4 | Representative case studies reveal distinct controller regimes

This figure uses PSE-derived structural panels to illustrate four distinct operating modes of the controller. The 1LBT panel represents a safety-preserving regime in which the incumbent solution is maintained despite active neural decoding. The TEM1 panel represents a stable-incumbent regime with a meaningful neural-decoder-derived learned alternative. The PETase panel illustrates reproducible redesign logic across two related backbones, showing that the controller can reproduce a chemically consistent solution rather than forcing novelty. The CLD panel acts as a calibration stress test, highlighting the tension between a stable incumbent and a nearby alternative favored by parts of the neural branch. Together, these panels demonstrate that `MARS-FIELD` does not operate identically across targets, but instead adapts to target-specific evidence structure and engineering context.
